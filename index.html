Chethiya Galkaduwa is a Computer Science PhD student at Indiana University Indianapolis (Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering) , advised by Prof. Mohamed Saeed Abdel-Mottaleb . Currently my research focuses on brain-inspired visual learning, local learning mechanisms in deep learning, computer vision , machine learning and biologically plausible learning rules for robust perception.

I bring experience from both academia and industry, including research at Purdue University (Kihara Lab), applied AI/ML consulting, and teaching roles in operating systems, algorithms, and programming. My interests center on visual attention, local learning mechanisms, and rigorous evaluation of models under domain shift.

My focus is on biologically inspired intelligence: bottom-up/top-down vision, recurrent inference, local learning rules, and robust evaluation under domain shift.
